## What is Ketamine and Its Uses in India?

Ketamine is a dissociative anesthetic primarily used in medical settings. Developed in the 1960s, it has gained prominence for its rapid-acting properties in surgery, pain management, and increasingly, mental health treatments like depression therapy.

In India, ketamine is regulated strictly due to its potential for misuse. Legally, it's available only through hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies with proper authorization. The Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) and Central Drugs Standard Control Organization (CDSCO) oversee its distribution to prevent abuse.

### Medical Applications of Ketamine

- **Anesthesia:** Commonly used in emergency procedures and veterinary medicine.
- **Pain Relief:** Effective for chronic pain conditions in controlled doses.
- **Mental Health:** Emerging as an off-label treatment for treatment-resistant depression, administered via infusions in specialized clinics.

As of 2026, India's healthcare landscape has seen a rise in ketamine-assisted psychotherapy, but access remains limited to certified facilities.

3. **Hospital Administration:** For therapeutic uses, ketamine is often given in-clinic via IV or nasal spray under supervision.

## Risks and Dangers of Unauthorized Ketamine Use

While ketamine has therapeutic value, non-medical use poses significant health and legal risks. In India, illicit sourcing often leads to impure products, increasing dangers like addiction, bladder damage, and psychosis.

### Common Hazards

- **Health Complications:** Overdose can cause respiratory failure or hallucinations.
- **Legal Consequences:** Possession without prescription can lead to up to 10 years in jail under NDPS.
- **Quality Issues:** Street or unregulated sources may contain contaminants.

Authorities like the Narcotics Control Bureau (NCB) actively monitor and crack down on illegal distribution networks.
